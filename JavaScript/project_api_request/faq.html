Frequently Asked Questions

A stock represents ownership in a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder and have the potential to profit from the company's success.

To buy stocks, you need to open a brokerage account with a reputable brokerage firm. Once your account is set up, you can place buy orders for the stocks you want to purchase.

A dividend is a payment made by a company to its shareholders, usually in the form of cash or additional shares. It is a portion of the company's profits distributed to the shareholders.

A stock market index is a measurement of the value of a specific section of the stock market. It represents the overall performance of a group of stocks and is used to track the market's performance.

A bull market refers to a period of time when the stock market is rising and investor confidence is high. It is characterized by increasing stock prices and optimism about the future.

A bear market refers to a period of time when the stock market is falling and investor confidence is low. It is characterized by decreasing stock prices and pessimism about the future.

You can minimize investment risks by diversifying your portfolio, conducting thorough research before investing, setting realistic expectations, and regularly reviewing and adjusting your investments.

A limit order is a type of order to buy or sell a stock at a specified price or better. It allows investors to set a specific price at which they are willing to buy or sell a stock, ensuring that the trade is executed at the desired price or better.

A market order is a type of order to buy or sell a stock at the current market price. It is executed immediately and guarantees that the trade will be executed, but the exact price at which the trade is executed may vary.

A stock split is a corporate action in which a company divides its existing shares into multiple shares. The number of shares increases, but the total value of the shares remains the same. Stock splits are often done to make the shares more affordable for investors.

A stock option is a financial instrument that gives the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell a specific number of shares of a company's stock at a predetermined price within a specified period of time. Stock options are often used as a form of compensation for employees or as investment vehicles for traders and investors.

A stock exchange is a marketplace where buyers and sellers trade stocks and other securities. It provides a platform for companies to raise capital by issuing stocks and for investors to buy and sell stocks. Stock exchanges play a crucial role in the functioning of the global financial system.
